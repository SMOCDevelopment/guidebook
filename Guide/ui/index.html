Getting Started

  1. Get your department uniform by typing /eup.
  2. Access your patrol vehicle by typing /vs.
  3. Press F11 to open the FivePD menu and go on duty. Then, navigate down a tab to equip your patrol loadout.
  4. Customize your vehicle by pressing F1, then go to Vehicle Options → Extras to toggle lightbars, push bars, and other equipment.

Additional Controls

  1. Use the X key to interact with AI pedestrians and vehicles during patrol.
  2. Press G to open the dispatch menu for requesting towing, EMS, coroner services, taxis, and more.
  3. When a call comes in, press Y to accept it. To deny a call, simply ignore it.
  4. Open your computer (MDT) by pressing B. You must be in your patrol vehicle and on duty to access it.
  5. Press Z to open the secondary menu for additional options like managing calls or other tasks.

Duties

  1. Patrol Officer: Respond to calls, perform traffic stops, and patrol zones.
  2. K9 Unit: Partner with a K9 for tracking and searches.
  3. Traffic Enforcement: Enforce traffic laws, perform DUI stops, and monitor speeding.
  4. SWAT: Handle high-risk situations (Requires Permission).

Please contact a Field Training Officer (FTO) or a senior officer if you are unsure about your duties or need assistance with a call.

Jurisdictions

  • County Units (Blaine County): Patrol rural and unincorporated areas of Blaine County.
  • City Units (Los Santos): Serve within the city limits of Los Santos.
  • State Units (State of San Andreas): Operate across the entire state, including highways and interstates.
  • Fire / EMS Units: Respond to medical emergencies and fires within your assigned department’s coverage area.
  • Federal Units: Operate under federal jurisdiction; stick to your assigned federal division responsibilities.

State Police Units - Please stick to the highways and interstates, as most units are assigned to a designated jurisdiction

Equipment

  • Radio: Always keep your radio on to maintain communication.
  • Weapons: Use only approved firearms and follow department guidelines.
  • Tools: Use spike strips, breathalyzers, and flashlights properly.

Maintain your equipment to ensure readiness at all times.

Communication

Radio Protocol

  • Radio Protocol: Use clear and concise language; identify yourself and your unit.
  • Codes: Familiarize yourself with department codes for efficient communication.
  • Emergency Calls: Prioritize emergency traffic and avoid unnecessary chatter.

Always confirm receipt of messages to avoid confusion.

Radio Frequencies

  • STATE: 1.23
  • COUNTY: 0.22
  • CITY: 4.9
  • FEDERAL: 9.00
  • FIRE/MEDICAL: 0.12
  • SADOT: 10.23
  • STAFF: 99.99

    This frequency is to get in touch with staff members. Use it only when necessary. Spamming this will result in a ban.

Rules

  • No RDM (Random Deathmatch): Killing players without proper roleplay is prohibited.
  • No VDM (Vehicle Deathmatch): Using vehicles to cause harm without roleplay is forbidden.
  • Stay In Character: Maintain your officer persona during duty at all times.
  • Radio Procedures: Follow all radio communication protocols and chain of command.
  • Respect Others: Treat all players with respect and professionalism.
  • Roleplay Realism: Follow realistic police procedures to enhance immersion.
